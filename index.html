My name is Holly, and I'm an orchestral conductor in one time zone, and a coder in another.

I'm a maker, a solutions-strategist, and a champion for kindness.

My Coding Life

In my own projects, I'm interested in the intersection of the arts and digital technology, and the possibilities for heightened creativity in real-world and networked spaces. I also have a PhD in Music Iconography, so have a geeky interested in the way musicians present themselves and their work, and are represented by others.

I'm passionate about smart solutions for a cleaner and fairer world, commissioning new creative work with an equitable focus, and skewing society towards seeing the arts as something to do rather than something to consume.

My two worlds - conducting and code - have much in common: architectural overview, strategic thinking and mid-flow problem solving. Where conducting sets itself apart is the unique challenge it poses of the synchronous - and time-locked - synthesis of critical, analytical and expressive activity. Oh, all while controlling every muscle, joint and facal expression... The upside is it means the asynchronous nature of logic, syntax and design that I enjoy in code feel like flying for me. I can let my brain go deep into one sphere of activity at a time, have a cup of tea whenever I need to take a step back and ponder something, and make my mistakes in private. Bliss.

My Musical Life

I am the Music Director for a cracking wee band in Kjipuktuk, Mi'kma'ki (Halifax, Nova Scotia - on the East Coast of Canada) called Symphony Nova Scotia. We work hard for many communities here, ranging from playing core classical and new works in the concert hall, to commissioning and presenting sell-out collaborations with Nova Scotian legends like Jah'mila, the Maritime Bhangra Group and "Mikmaltic" fiddler, Morgan Toney. We take large- and small-scale live art music to schools, museums, libraries and provincial halls, we livestream much of our work, and we make quirky little things with community partners, like rad videos for Art Gallery of Nova Scotia, pairing works in their exhibitions with pieces of music. Check out our YouTube channel to see some of our work.

I'm also co-chair of the Board of Directors for FormidAbility, Artist-in-Association at English Touring Opera, and when time permits around being a mum, coding and watering my plants back in glasgow, I take on a small number of guest conducting gigs. But these days, it has to be a pretty interesting project or some spectacularly lovely people for me to prioritise it over other interests and commitments.

If you have a project that would benefit from a right-brain strategist with left-brain creativity - drop me a line!
