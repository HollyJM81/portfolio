About me

My name is Holly, and I'm an orchestral conductor by day, and a web and software developer by night.

I'm a maker, a solutions-strategist, and a champion for kindness.

In my own projects, I'm interested in the intersection of music and digital technology, and the possibilities for heightened creativity in real-world and networked spaces. I also have a PhD in Music Iconography, so have a geeky interested in the way musicians present themselves and their work, and are represented by others.

I'm passionate about smart solutions for a cleaner and fairer world, commissioning new creative work with an equitable focus, and skewing society towards seeing the arts as something to do rather than something to consume.

My two worlds - conducting and code - have much in common: architectural overview, strategic thinking, and mid-flow problem solving. Where conducting sets itself apart is the unique challenge it poses of the synchronous, and time-locked, synthesis of critical, analytical and expressive activity. Oh, all while controlling every muscle, joint and facal expression... The upside is it means the asynchronous nature of logic, syntax and design that I enjoy in code feel like flying for me. I can let my brain go deep into one sphere of activity at a time, have a cup of tea whenever I need to take a step back and ponder something, and make my mistakes in private. Bliss.


If you would like to work with a developer who prioritises rigorous structure and accessibility, left-field-creative energy, and a collaborative working style - drop me a line!
